    Project Idea
    Overview

    An application for support during puberty. The application is aimed at anyone going through puberty and the unknown areas regarding the topic and other areas of support.

    The project is an application for the mobile market and is a progressional step towards puberty as the target audience will always be there and the requirement for this area is becoming more progressively mainstream but due to the fact that this area of growing up still holds some social stigmas persons of the age group may find puberty confusing and may seek guidance that isn't necessarily there or the persons may be in a remote location without access to services to answer questions. This project is formed around the familiar generational technological environment that the target audience is drawn towards.

    The application shall include support and information for people who are having a hard time or otherwise cannot find the required information from other sources. The Application will have an incognito mode that allows for the application to be exited when the user requires a fast exit. The application will also allow for the update of information via a database on a cloud server this gives the application the added benefit of being able to be current with trends.


    Topic

    What is proposed for this project is to create an application that can utilise information and to deliver it in a fun way. The project is an application and the idea is to have the application created to function as is as well as allow for changes when new information is available. The application is to be simple and easy to exit on request. The application will be sent out as a free application.

    Adding to the outcomes the project wishes to be a data collaboration of information that is easy to access and could be recommended to services who deal with the target audience. The application is to be frank and honest and not shy away from social stigmas of the subjects involved.

    Motivation

    Information relating to puberty and other related health matters for many people can be hard to find, incomplete or simply non existent. There are many people that find they have questions that they are often too embarrassed to ask, find it difficult to raise with family members or avoid asking for fear of ridicule or ill treatment. This app aims to provide information and cover a broad range of puberty related topics for people of all ages, genders and sexual orientations in a single complete reference platform. Many of us have experienced limited knowledge of what lies ahead with regard to puberty and adulthood, most of us have learnt and gained experience by having to learn the hard way, or spend a lot of time researching or simply doing things wrong.

    All of us at one time or another would’ve appreciated having the information at a point in our lives and having it all in one place and easy to access would be advantageous for many. Helping to path the direction of the culture of adults who are informed can shape the way society as a whole will function in future generations to come knowledge is power the motivation it to help.

    The motivation is to inform people regarding puberty and other health matters. The project is one that is motivated by the existence of information that can be hard to find or to raise with people's families or support network.

    The project is interesting as it aims to work with the progression of society and tackle the normilisation of transgender, lesbian and gay and other aspects of the person prefrence by not shaming young people who need advice.
    Furthermore, the app is intended to provide a better understanding of ourselves. The motive behind this particular idea is that accessing information relating to puberty and other related health matters for many people can be hard to find, incomplete or simply non existent. Puberty App has all of the above while supporting through those phases, often questions that are raised can be embarrassing and uncomfortable to bring up accessing those information is at reach. Normalising this idea will create a positive and open environment for upcoming employees.

    Landscape

    The landscape of the puberty applications show apps that are two gender specific or religious based and not showing the science/social based approach on sexual identities and gender assignment. This application aims for transparency in these topics and to be able to incorporate a factual basis for the information.

    Description

    Within the program of this ‘Puberty App’ key activities, facts and tips will be included; this describes the transformation that happens with estrogen, testosterone , gender transforming surgeries, sexual health and any other needs that come in later updates with the server based cloud idea of the project.

    As a basics this application is a source of information for all kinds of people who are going through puberty or who just need the information by itself. There will be a homepage with an option to create an account and save the different pages that they will want to access later on. This account will be saved to the cloud so it can be accessed from other devices, for the moment it will just be mobiles, however, it will eventually expand to websites as well. Once the account has been created (or they login as guest) there will be spaces to add in their age, gender identification, sexual orientation and specific pronouns. These details will help to give options and suggest reading articles for the user. Each section will give the details in a fun and interactive manner so it is easier for neurodivergent people to read and take in.

    The sections will be as followed:

    For anyone undergoing transformation through estrogen (whether that be cisgender or transfeminine) there will be information on:

    • Bras; sizing, different types (Underwired, push-up, balconette, bralette, strapless, tube, sports, t-shirt, beginner’s, transparent, bridal, multiway, plunge, cage, halter, nursing, stick-on, full figure, padded, minimizer and racerback )
    • Sexual health care; birth control (both the pill and the rod), condoms (why they’re important and how to use one)
    • Periods; how they work and what they do, pads and tampons (the pros and cons for both and the different brands)
    • Mood swings; the most common moods associated with estrogen.

    Anyone undergoing transformation through testosterone (whether that be cisgender or transmasculine) there will be information on:

    • Development of testicles “balls dropping”.
    • Wet dreams.
    • Erections; their function and general information.
    • Sexual health care; vasectomy (function and information) and condoms (how and when to use and what they do).
    • Voice breaks.
    • Mood swings; the most common moods associated with testosterone.

    For both estrogen and testosterone there will also be information on:

    • Shaving; face, legs, under arms and chest (razors information, how-to videos, and step-by-step instructions), hair removal (waxing, cream, shaving cream, laser, epilators, plucking).
    • Skin care routines; creams, moisturizers, anti-acne etc. (different brands and uses).
    • The LGBTQIA+ area will include information for both transfeminine and transmasculine.

    For transfeminine it will include:

    • Padding; sizing and how-to (padded bras and underwear)
    • Body shaping; types (control top pantyhose, camisole, waist cincher)
    • Gaffs; types (underwear, adhesive tape)
    • Hormone effects (changes to face, increase in chest, decrease in genitals, changing of body shape, voice, mood swings)
    • Surgeries; top surgery (breast implants), bottom surgery (Penile inversion, rectosigmoid or colon graft, non-penile inversion vaginoplasty).

    For transmasculine it will include:

    • Binders; sizing and types (half, full, t-shirt, singlet, swimming)
    • Packers; sizing and types (STP, soft, hard)
    • Hormone effects (changes to face, reduction of chest, changing of body shape, voice, bottom growth, mood swings)
    • Surgeries; top surgery (Buttonhole, double incision, inverted-T/T-anchor, peri-areolar, keyhole, minimal scar), bottom surgery (Metoidioplasty, phalloplasty).

    For both gender identification and sexual orientation there will be information on:

    • Different types of gender identification (Cisgender, transgender, gender nonconforming, nonbinary, genderqueer, genderfluid, gender-neutral, intersex)
    • Different types of sexual orientation (Straight, gay, lesbian, bisexual, pansexual, asexual, demisexual, graysexual)
    • How to come out to your parents.
    • What to do if your parents don’t accept what you say.
    • LGBTQIA+ lines.

    Another part of this app will be the quick exit button somewhere on the page (in a corner) for anyone who does not want to be ‘caught’ on the app or reading something not allowed. This button will either completely close down the app or will switch the user’s mobile over to another app (chosen by the user). A feature like this allows for a quick exit in case of emergency such as; parents or guardians who do not want their children looking into LGBTQIA+ activities, almost being caught by a partner when the user does not want them to know etc. The quick exit button is for any user who does not wish for anyone to know they are looking at any of this information to protect their privacy.

    Plans Stages

    The following is a plan of action that is proposed to be done (or completed) and the details of the stage in the project. The current stage that the project is upto is reflected in the creation of mock-up being stage 2 in the following plan.

    Stage 1 - Create the Plan - This stage creates the idea of the plan including the target audience, content and other information needed for the project. This is the brainstorming stage.

    Stage 2 - Create Application Mock-up - This requires creating a mock-up to demonstrate the application is a non-functional or basic functional artefact to show what the app may look like prior to the end agreed finish of graphics and resources.

    Stage 3 - Obtain resources - Gather information on the resources used in the application as well as services that are required for the function of the application. This requires a research process and projects as well as setting up of services.

    Stage 4 - Development of application - Application starts to be programmed from plans and the formation of the information is amalgamated into the development.

    Stage 5 - Test plan and risk management plans - Creation of documents for testing and the risk management plan for the delivery of the application to market. Important phase for the completion of this stage can run alongside the development of the application.

    Stage 6 - Completion of application version 1 - Complete the application for the first trial to market. This is the stage in which all final inputs are to be done and any remaining ideas or functions can be placed into the next stage.

    Stage 7- Ongoing support plan - Creation of supporting patches, documents and other planned upgrades. This stage is where the next lot of ideas, innovations and any other upgrades or responses to feedback are considered or executed.

    Stages are linear and the project runs via the plan but the mitigation of unforeseen circumstances may cause delays and some stages might be able to work within one another to accomplish an end result.

    The plan we would like to adopt is that of an “Agile” approach that will allow for the project to be flexible and adhere to industry standards of project management within this sort of project type.

    Risks

    Risks that can pose a threat to the project are the following and the team have acknowledged some factors that might need to be mitigated when possible including the risk likeliness.

    The following risks may arise is the nature of a our project:

    • Broken Links - The application relies on information and a broken link would risk the functionality of the application.
    • Scope Creep - This could be a risk if there are parts of the project that are not estimated such as time to create application or resource delays such as information.
    • Poor risk management - Risk management in the software development regarding the teams used to spot any potential issues make for comprehensive risk assessment. If the team has poor risk then creating mitigation is not efficient.
    • Incapable software - With the advent of mobile technology the application may not function on some devices this needs to be mitigated by way of using older versions of software. The hardest task is that new devices may make older software obsolete.
    • Target Audience - A risk to this project is if the application can get to its target audience. The success of the project depends on enough users downloading the application.

    The following mitigation chart shows the following Keys using 3 coloured stages.

    • Green - Low risk - Can be mitigated or poses an insignificant issue to the project.
    • Orange - Medium risk - Mitigation should be planned and all parties advised and any plans revised or amended.
    • Red. - High risk - A risk is not controllable through mitigation attempts i.e. third party service denial or server crash.

    Risk Likeliness Mitigation
    Broken Links Low 5% Checking links can be done via app. This can be mitigated with saving content to a database
    Scope Creep Low 5% Can be mitigated by revising plans. 

    Poor Risk Management Medium 25% This is a medium threat as motivation of teams can cause issues. This needs to be addressed by way of regular role changes and incentives.
    Incompatable Software Medium 20% Mitigation of incompatible software is hard to mitigate but the update of the application platform with templating would assist.
    Target Audience Low 3% Target audience can be mitigated by using feedback for future application features.

    Service Issues High 50% This is a situation that is out of control, mitigation would be difficult. The only real mitigation is to try to offer an offline service that is as impactful as the online service of the project.
